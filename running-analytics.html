Contributing Factors
Why the injury happened — and how to prevent recurrence
📈
Excessive Volume Spike
7 consecutive weeks >80km (Jul 21–Sep 8). 4 weeks over 100km. No true recovery week — "deload" Aug 11 was still 77km. Cumulative bone stress with no remodeling time.
⏱
Intensity + Volume Stacked
Track Tuesdays + Heartbreak Hill workouts in the same weeks as 100km+ totals. High stress both mechanically and metabolically with no buffer weeks.
🦴
Bone Density Risk
Osteopenia at femoral neck (T-score -1.2). Lower bone mineral density means higher fracture/stress reaction risk under repetitive impact loading at extreme volumes.
☀
Vitamin D Deficiency
43 ng/mL in Aug 2025 (goal: 50–60 ng/mL). Vitamin D is essential for calcium absorption and bone remodeling. Deficiency during peak load = impaired repair.
🏃
Post-Marathon Recovery Gap
London Marathon Apr 27, 2025. Volume rebuilt quickly after — body was in long-term recovery deficit from two marathons before the summer build began.
⚡
Relative Energy Deficiency
REDs/low energy availability history. At high training volumes, insufficient caloric intake impairs bone adaptation and hormonal function, compounding stress fracture risk.
✅ Prevention Protocol for NYC Build
• True deload every 4th week (≤60% of peak volume)  •  Max 10% weekly increase  •  Vitamin D target 55+ ng/mL
• Track workouts are great for marathon performance — hold off during Phase 1–3 (until cleared by Dr. Best), then embrace them fully  •  Prioritize 8–9 hrs sleep during high volume weeks (90km+)  •  Fueling audit at marathon pace training
